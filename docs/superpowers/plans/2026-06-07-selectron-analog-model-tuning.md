## 0. Headline / what this plan is and is not

This plan tightens the **analog-habitat** risk model (`analog-controlled`, `antarctic-station`) to the
evidence, and remediates the citation-integrity and scientific-substance problems the peer review
found in the crew-science extension. It is **forward-looking**: it affects the *next* (analog)
manuscript iteration, not the ASR R4 submission package, which the review confirmed is clean.

**The one trap this plan deliberately avoids.** The reflex response to "model the time of isolation"
is to bolt a duration-amplifier onto the psychological hazard. We checked whether a primary source —
or even this repo's own `src/risk/` NHPP fit — provides a *quantified* psych-rate-vs-duration
relationship. It does not (see §4). Building a new evidence-labeled amplifier on a cumulative-onset
statistic (Bell 2019's "≥1 conflict by 40%/90 d") would **reproduce the exact error class the peer
review just caught** (§3.1/§3.5: a parametric amplifier on thin evidence, over-claimed). So Theme C
is "drop the refuted amplifier, keep the model linear-in-duration, and document the rate-rise as a
limitation" — not "build a ramp."

### Hard invariants (write these into every commit; a future session must not undo them)

1. **`leo-iss` / K15 stays byte-identical.** Every new mechanism is gated on
   `isTerrestrialAnalog(mission.kind)` and/or `member.stageAScores` presence. The K15 invariance
   canary in `tests/imm/simulate.test.ts` is the guardrail.
2. **`paper/` submission package is untouched.** All work is branch-isolated for the analog
   manuscript iteration. The ASR R4 `.docx` are not regenerated by this plan.
3. **No DOI is used until it resolves.** Every identifier in `research/evidence_extracted/` must
   resolve in Crossref and match title/authors before its value justifies a coefficient or sentence
   (the §3.8 process gate from the review). One `get_crossref_paper_by_doi` per citation.
4. **Math/tests before UI** (repo rule); **update `STATUS.md` in the same commit** (resume protocol).
5. **ISS path and `src/engine/` MCDA primitives are out of scope** — frozen at the K15 reproduction.

### Two problem classes (different remedies, per review §0)

- **Citation integrity** → re-ground to real sources or delete (Phase 0).
- **Scientific substance** → design reconsideration, not just re-citation (Phases 2–4).

## Phase 2 — Theme A: Selection/training gradient (random people vs. trained-and-selected crew)

This is the **strongest new theme** and the most direct answer to Diego's framing. It is grounded in
verified material. Diego asked for *time of isolation* **and** *selected-vs-random* as one coupled
phenomenon — so the guard is against **inventing** a new interaction amplifier, **not** against
modelling the interaction at all. The bridge already exists, fit, in-repo (see **A3**); use it rather
than building a fresh one. Basner Mars-500 heterogeneity remains suggestive-only and is not used as a
quantified interaction term.

Three literature-grounded effects:

**A1 — Selection-process baseline floor (psych/behavioral incidence).**
A screened, trained crew sits at the Palinkas & Suedfeld 2008 **~5% DSM floor**; a random/unscreened
population approaches general-population 12-month prevalence (~20–30% any disorder; ~5–10% severe).
The defensibility review §1/§5 already establishes this gradient and frames the ~11× screened-vs-
unscreened ratio as a **bounding sensitivity**, not a real-cohort estimate. This baseline floor is
**verifiable and PyMC-fittable** from the Palinkas / general-prevalence counts.

**A2 — Situational-strength β-dampening (review §3.4).**
In mature, highly-trained programs with strong safety climate, the **trait→outcome coupling is
attenuated**: a trained crew with identical trait scores carries weaker coupling than a random crew.
Anchors to verify and cite: Lee et al. 2016; Wilmot & Ones 2019; Doerr 2020 (each must pass the
Phase 0 gate). The **Palinkas 2000 boundary condition** (low-C predicted *better* Antarctic
performance in a mature 1960s–70s program) lives here as the limiting case, not as an inversion of
the modern meta-analytic picture.

**A3 — Selection × time via the latent-class mix (the literal time-AND-selection bridge).**
`src/risk/` already encodes the coupling Diego is describing, and it leans on a **fit** quantity, not
an invented one: the Tu 2024 latent-class split `π_unstable` (133/202, fitted) divides crews into a
**stable/flat** trajectory and an **unstable/back-loaded-rising** one (`temporal.ts`: class 1 = rising
over mission fraction), and the B5 PPC harness already checks that the **unstable fraction is lower for
high-fit crews**. So selection/training acts on the **class mixture**: trained-and-selected → lower
P(unstable) → flatter trajectory; random → higher P(unstable) → risk that rises in the back half of
the confinement. This is the time×selection interaction the user asked for, expressed through an
existing fitted proportion rather than a new amplifier — fully consistent with the Theme C redirect.
Action: wire selection/training to shift the latent-class mix (keep it in `src/risk`, where it lives);
the **selection→mix magnitude** is operator-supplied (same disclosure class as A2), the **mix split
itself stays Tu-2024-anchored**. The manuscript↔pipeline routing tension (the paper reports the linear
IMM; this bridge lives in `src/risk`) is resolved **deliberately in Phase 7**, not papered over.

**Mechanism (analog-gated):** a per-crew/mission **selection-training context** that (i) sets the
psych/behavioral baseline multiplier (A1), (ii) scales the trait β by a situational-strength
factor < 1 for trained crews (A2), and (iii) shifts the `src/risk` latent-class mix toward the stable
trajectory for trained crews (A3). Gated on `isTerrestrialAnalog(kind)` → K15 untouched.

**Per-kind nuance:** `antarctic-station` = occupationally selected national-program winter-overs
(Palinkas data applies directly); `analog-controlled` = volunteer/selected analog astronauts
(MDRS / HI-SEAS / Mars-500).

**Calibration:** A1 floor → PyMC fit (verifiable counts). A2 dampening magnitude → operator-supplied
tuning parameter, disclosed (Phase 5), ideally sensitivity-swept.

> **Ratification fork F1:** representation of the selection axis — a binary
> `selectionContext: "screened-trained" | "unscreened-random"`, or a continuous
> `programMaturity ∈ [0,1]`. Recommendation: binary first (matches the bounding-analysis framing the
> lit reviews endorse), continuous later if a graded anchor appears.

## Phase 3 — Theme B: Agreeableness / interpersonal coupling (review §3.2 gap)

The current IMM crew-coupling uses **only conscientiousness** (`computeCrewSafetyClimateMultiplier`).
Three verified meta-analyses show **agreeableness is at least as strong** a safety/team predictor
(Clarke 2005 A .26 ≈ C .27; Beus 2014 — agreeableness the largest variance share in safety behavior,
*verify*; Peeters 2006 A ρ=0.24 > C 0.20; Bell 2007 team min-agreeableness strongest). For I&C crews,
team cohesion is dominated by interpersonal compatibility, so this gap matters more, not less.

**Mechanism:** no `psych.agreeableness` criterion exists — `behavioral.teamwork` is the
interpersonal-compatibility proxy (state this as a **proxy, not an identity**). Add crew-level coupling
on `behavioral.teamwork` **parallel to** the conscientiousness path, on the
behavioral / traumatic / interpersonal-conflict families. Bell 2007 supports the **team-minimum**
operationalization (weakest-link) — state it as *one* defensible choice (mirroring the corrected Xu
framing), not as "the literature singles out minimum."

Gated analog-only + `stageAScores` presence → K15 untouched. β magnitude: operator-supplied tuning
unless a primary regression gives a per-condition coefficient (Phase 5).

> **Ratification fork F3:** couple via existing `behavioral.teamwork` (recommended — already a Tier
> criterion) vs. add a new first-class `psych.agreeableness` criterion.

## Phase 4 — Theme C: Time of isolation & confinement (the corrected approach)

**C1 — Drop or demote the third-quarter mode.** Phase B (`thirdQuarterMode` / `THIRD_QUARTER_C_AMP =
1.4`) rests on a **contested-to-refuted** phenomenon (Bell 2019, Hawkes 2017, Skorupa 2024, Kanas 2021
all refute; its own cited anchor argues against it). It is **opt-in, defaults false, and is wired into
no UI or production path** — removing it changes **no reported result**. Either delete the
`THIRD_QUARTER_*` path, or **demote it to an explicitly-labeled speculative operator toggle (default
off)** with the refuting evidence cited in-code/UI/dossier (review P1.6). Recommendation: delete from
the default model; keep at most a clearly-labeled scenario toggle.

**C2 — Keep incidence linear-in-duration; the time axis is already honored correctly.**
- Cumulative P(≥1 medical event) → near-certain by ≥45 d (defensibility review Assumption 2,
  **well-supported**: JARE ~4 events/person; ANARE ~6.6/person-year). The linear-in-duration Poisson
  already produces this.
- pEVAC **superlinearity** comes from the **kit-depletion (RAF) mechanism**, which is mechanistically
  grounded (Antonsen 2022; defensibility review §3) — **not** from a psychological amplifier. This is
  the legitimate, evidence-backed source of duration-dependence and it already exists.

**C3 — Document the rate-rise as an explicit limitation (do not ship it as a mechanism).** The per-day
psych/behavioral hazard *may* rise with cumulative confinement time; the IMM (like NASA's own model)
treats psych incidence as **constant-rate**. Antonsen 2022 flags this qualitatively but (per the
repo's lit-reviews) provides **no quantified psych-incidence-by-duration curve** — its duration-
stratified data are EVAC/LOCL. State this as a scope limitation (review P1.6; kit-training review §4.2)
rather than inventing a curve. **Confirm against the Antonsen 2022 primary text in Phase 1** before the
sentence enters the manuscript limitation paragraph — that characterization currently rests on the
repo's *secondary* lit-review, not the primary; the linear choice is conservative either way, so this
verifies the wording, it does not gate the decision.

**C4 — `src/risk/` reconciliation (defensibility, not refactor).** `conflict_fit.py` confirms the
back-loaded NHPP ramp shape params **`temporal_a` / `temporal_p` are synthetic defaults, not fit**;
`λ_base` was derived from Bell 2019 under a **constant-hazard** inversion. So the super-linearity in
`src/risk/` is **assumed**. Action: (a) relabel `temporal_a/p` "operator-supplied tuning parameters"
in code + dossier; (b) add a **sensitivity sweep over (a, p)** so the back-loading is reported as a
modeled assumption with bounds; (c) **keep** the Tu 2024 latent-class split (`π_unstable` *is* fit).
Do not present the back-loading magnitude as evidence-based. (Per CLAUDE.md, do not couple the two
pipelines' code — but the duration *science* must be consistent across them; an inconsistent duration
model across pipelines is itself a defensibility liability.)

> **Ratification fork F2:** third-quarter — delete entirely vs. demote to a labeled speculative
> toggle (default off).
