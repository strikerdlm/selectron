# PJAMBP Author-Guideline Digest

Target journal: *The Polish Journal of Aviation Medicine, Bioengineering and Psychology*

Sources:

- Journal information: http://pjambp.com/page/display/id/1/title/strona-glowna
- Author information: http://pjambp.com/page/display/id/2/title/informacje-dla-autorow
- ISSN Portal: https://portal.issn.org/resource/ISSN/2451-3512

## Scope

PJAMBP publishes work in aviation medicine, space medicine, bioengineering, psychology, human factors, flight safety, occupational health, and complex operational environments. This makes the journal a plausible home for a methodology paper that connects analog-astronaut selection, aerospace-medicine risk modeling, and decision-support communication.

## Article Type

Use **Original research**.

The manuscript should be framed as a reproducible aerospace-medicine decision-support methodology, not as a deployed operational selector, clinical certification tool, or NASA-endorsed risk standard.

## Length and Structure

- Original research: up to 6000 words excluding bibliography, tables, and figures.
- Structured abstract: up to 250 words.
- Suggested abstract headings: Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion and conclusions.
- Keywords: 3-7 terms, preferably MeSH.
- Main text: Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion, Conclusions.

## Style

- English manuscripts are accepted.
- References should follow the journal's modified Vancouver format; DOI is encouraged.
- A consistent reference style is acceptable at initial submission, but the DOCX should be built with Vancouver numbering.
- Figures and tables may be embedded near first mention.
- Figure files should be publication quality; 300 dpi is the practical target for raster images.

## Fees and Access

The journal states that there are no article submission, processing, or publication charges.

## AI Policy

AI cannot be listed as an author. Authors retain full responsibility for content. If AI-generated content is used as manuscript content, it should be disclosed. AI-assisted editing, language improvement, translation, idea generation, or visualization support does not require formal disclosure when the final work is author-developed, but the existing manuscript AI statement is conservative and can be retained.

## Submission Package Implications

- Provide a blinded or non-blinded manuscript as requested by the submission route.
- Provide a title page/cover information separately if requested.
- Provide a cover letter that explicitly names the journal scope: aviation and space medicine, bioengineering, psychology, human factors, and flight safety.
- Do not overstate clinical or operational readiness.
- Keep the paper under the 6000-word limit by moving detailed V&V, prior tables, and reproducibility locks to the repository/supplement.
