To the Editors of *The Polish Journal of Aviation Medicine, Bioengineering and Psychology*

Dear Editors,

I am submitting the manuscript **"Bayesian Aerospace-Medicine Decision Support for Analog-Astronaut Selection and Mission-Risk Mapping"** for consideration as an original research article.

The manuscript presents Selectron, a reproducible decision-support methodology for analog-astronaut and isolated-confined-environment crew selection. The work fits PJAMBP's scope because it combines aviation and space medicine, human factors, psychology, bioengineering, and flight-safety risk communication. The paper addresses a practical aerospace-medicine problem: selection panels often reduce heterogeneous candidate criteria to deterministic rankings, while analog mission-risk assessments are difficult to compare across programs.

Selectron addresses this problem in two linked stages. Stage A models selection-criterion weights with a Dirichlet prior and reports posterior candidate-score uncertainty. Stage B implements a NASA Integrated Medical Model-aligned Monte Carlo mission medical-risk simulator and maps Crew Health Index and mission-success probability to the NASA HSRB Likelihood x Consequence matrix. The manuscript reports internal verification, K15 reference-crew inter-model agreement, and transparent limitations.

The manuscript is deliberately framed as a research and risk-communication method. It is not presented as a clinical tool, flight-certification system, operational astronaut-selection system, or NASA-endorsed risk-governance product. No human subjects, clinical records, personnel files, or analog-mission incident registries were collected or analyzed. The examples use synthetic candidate data and published reference configurations.

The work is original, has not been published elsewhere, and is not under consideration by another journal. I am the sole author and declare no competing interests. No external funding was received. The Selectron software artifact is MIT-licensed and archived at `https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20693257`, with source code available at `https://github.com/strikerdlm/selectron`.
