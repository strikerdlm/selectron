# International Aerospace-Medicine Journal Scout

Date: 2026-06-21

Scope requested: international/non-US aerospace medicine journals discoverable through Google Scholar or a scholarly database. US journals, including *Aerospace Medicine and Human Performance*, were excluded.

## Recommendation

**Selected target: The Polish Journal of Aviation Medicine, Bioengineering and Psychology (PJAMBP).**

PJAMBP is the best match for this manuscript because its stated scope combines aviation and space medicine with bioengineering, psychology, flight safety, occupational health, human factors, and complex operational environments. That combination fits Selectron better than a strictly clinical aeromedical journal: the manuscript is a decision-support, medical-risk simulation, and human-factors methodology paper rather than a case series or aircrew clinical standards paper.

The main tradeoff is indexing strength. PJAMBP is discoverable through Google Scholar, CrossRef, Index Copernicus, PBN, and ROAD/ISSN, but I did not find MEDLINE, PubMed, Scopus, or EMBASE coverage. If database indexing is more important than manuscript-fit and word-limit feasibility, the strongest alternate is the **Indian Journal of Aerospace Medicine**, which is non-US, English, no-fee, and lists EMBASE/Excerpta Medica plus Google Scholar/CrossRef, but it caps original articles at 3000 words.

## Why PJAMBP Wins

PJAMBP's author guidance allows original research up to 6000 words, which is the only realistic limit among the exact aerospace-medicine journals found for this manuscript without destroying the methodological argument. The journal also explicitly welcomes work at the intersection of medicine, psychology, and bioengineering, which aligns with Selectron's Bayesian MCDA, medical-risk simulation, and HSRB risk-communication bridge.

The journal's AI policy is also workable for this paper: AI cannot be listed as an author, authors remain responsible for content, generated manuscript content must be disclosed, and AI-assisted editing/style/language work does not require formal disclosure if the final content is author-developed. The existing manuscript already has an AI-use statement that can be retained and tightened.

## PJAMBP Author Requirements Relevant to This Submission

- Article type: Original research.
- Language: English accepted.
- Length: original articles up to 6000 words, excluding bibliography, tables, and figures.
- Abstract: structured, up to 250 words, with headings such as Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion and conclusions.
- Keywords: 3-7 keywords, preferably MeSH.
- References: modified Vancouver; DOI encouraged; consistent format accepted at initial submission.
- Figures/tables: embedded near first mention; figures should be high-resolution, generally 300 dpi.
- Peer review: journal describes peer review; page wording includes a blind-review policy.
- Charges: no article submission, processing, or publication fees.
- Submission: JournalsTube or email route is described on the author page.

## Alternate Route: IJASM

IJASM is the best alternate if the editor or author prioritizes database indexing over manuscript length. It is an exact aerospace-medicine journal, English language, open access, no author fee, and lists EMBASE/Excerpta Medica plus Google Scholar/CrossRef. The main reason it is not the selected target is feasibility: the 3000-word cap for original articles would require a much more severe conversion into a short methods note, likely with most validation details moved to supplement.
