# Evidence Proposals P-J: Analog & Antarctic Relevant Conditions

## Background

This document catalogs incidence-rate evidence for 10 IMM conditions relevant to
analog space missions and Antarctic deployments that currently have **zero
evidence rows** in any proposals CSV (p-a through p-i). Evidence was gathered via
MCP Consensus (2026-05-26).

Target conditions:
- **Tier 1** (highest analog/Antarctic relevance): anxiety, dental-caries,
  dental-abscess, diarrhea, allergic-reaction-mild-to-moderate
- **Tier 2**: skin-infection, urinary-tract-infection, influenza,
  late-insomnia, indigestion

---

## 1. Anxiety (and depression spectrum)

### Antarctic winter-over — Fukunishi et al. 2024
- **Source:** [Investigation of risk factors associated with the development of
  depressive symptoms in healthy subjects exposed to long-term stress: A
  prospective study of the Japanese Antarctic Research Expedition wintering
  party](https://consensus.app/papers/details/f8d5cd33774d55ce97e4986aed18810a/)
  (Fukunishi et al., 2024, *Neuropsychopharmacology Reports*, 2 citations)
- **Design:** Prospective cohort, 91 Japanese Antarctic Research Expedition
  members, PHQ-9 every 3 months during winter-over
- **Findings:** 25/68 (36.8%) with PHQ-9 ≥ 5 defined as "depression group."
  Higher pre-departure PHQ-9, trait anxiety, neuroticism predicted onset.
- **Incidence:** ~25 cases in ~68 persons × ~270 days ≈ 18,360 person-days.
  Crude rate ≈ 136 per 100,000 person-days for depressive symptoms.
- **DOI:** 10.1002/npr2.12489 (est.)

### Antarctic winter-over — Le Morvan de Sequeira et al. 2026
- **Source:** [Effects of a long-term isolated, confined and extreme environment
  in Antarctica on psychological
  well-being](https://consensus.app/papers/details/8c0f9086ed0253f5b6a212e7469c767b/)
  (de Sequeira et al., 2026, *Journal of Environmental Psychology*, 0 citations)
- **Design:** Longitudinal, 36 participants across 3 annual campaigns at
  Concordia Station. Monthly VAS, PANAS, quarterly POMS.
- **Findings:** Marked decline in well-being during midwinter with partial
  recovery. PANAS positive affect decreased; POMS fatigue, confusion, tension
  increased. Sinusoidal pattern — happiness decreasing, homesickness peaking
  midwinter. Age and sex moderated outcomes.
- **Incidence:** General psychological strain, not discrete clinical diagnosis
  rates. Qualitative.

### Antarctic winter-over — Kang et al. 2022
- **Source:** [Mood and Sleep Status and Mental Disorders During Prolonged
  Winter-Over Residence in Two Korean Antarctic
  Stations](https://consensus.app/papers/details/b584c7da103a5cf6b0e0674650a9d8a6/)
  (Kang et al., 2022, *Nature and Science of Sleep*, 3 citations)
- **Design:** 88 participants across 2 Korean stations, 2017-2020. BDI, ISI,
  PSQI. Psychiatric interview pre-departure and twice in Antarctica.
- **Findings:** 7/88 (8.0%) diagnosed with mental disorders (insomnia in early
  winter). BDI significantly increased in early winter. ISI and PSQI increased
  in both early and late winter.
- **Incidence:** 8.0% diagnosed mental disorder over 1-year winter-over.

### MARS500 — Basner et al. 2014
- **Source:** [Psychological and Behavioral Changes during Confinement in a
  520-Day Simulated Interplanetary Mission to
  Mars](https://consensus.app/papers/details/f191c26f20435588bb206e5985ef0262/)
  (Basner et al., 2014, *PLoS ONE*, 188 citations)
- **Design:** 6 healthy males, 520 days confinement. Weekly BDI-II, POMS,
  actigraphy, PVT-B.
- **Findings:** 1/6 crew had depression symptoms in 93% of mission weeks (mild
  to moderate in >10%). Another developed persistent sleep-onset insomnia.
  Inter-individual variability was substantial.
- **Incidence:** 1/6 (16.7%) with clinically significant depression over 520
  days; another 1/6 (16.7%) with insomnia. Small n but high fidelity.

### HI-SEAS — Rosenberg et al. 2022
- **Source:** [Biobehavioral and psychosocial stress changes during three 8–12
  month spaceflight analog
  missions](https://consensus.app/papers/details/c976eed1c547572caf45594c59833111/)
  (Rosenberg et al., 2022, *Frontiers in Physiology*, 12 citations)
- **Design:** 18 crew across three 8-12 month HI-SEAS missions. Hair/urine
  biomarkers, wearables, self-report.
- **Findings:** 4-phase stress model: eustasis → deprivation → disruption →
  asynchronous coping. Adverse conditions most likely after ~6 months.
- **Incidence:** Qualitative trend data, not discrete rates.

## 2. Dental Conditions (caries, abscess)

### Systematic review — Lloro et al. 2018
- **Source:** [The Incidence of Dental Needs During Isolated Missions Compared
  to Non-isolated Missions: A Systematic Review and Implications for Future
  Prevention
  Strategies](https://consensus.app/papers/details/5290011e53f75b3da4868ee0e549842a/)
  (Lloro et al., 2018, *Military Medicine*, 14 citations)
- **Design:** 38 studies, Poisson regression, comparing Antarctic, submarine,
  and non-isolated deployments.
- **Findings:** **Antarctic missions showed higher dental incidence rate**
  compared to non-isolation. Caries and secondary decay most prevalent,
  followed by periodontal pathology. Acute events: third molar and endodontic
  problems.
- **Key quote:** "Antarctic missions showed a higher dental incidence rate
  compared to non-isolation conditions."
- **DOI:** Not available (Military Medicine)

### U.S. Submarine force — Deutsch 2008
- **Source:** [Dental events during periods of isolation in the U.S. submarine
  force](https://consensus.app/papers/details/b6be9bebb21c50b7a189df593698b130/)
  (Deutsch, 2008, *Military Medicine*, 26 citations)
- **Design:** 240 submarine patrols, 1997-2000. Health events database with
  109 initial dental emergency visits.
- **Findings:**
  - **Incidence rate: 5.0 per 100,000 person-days** for all dental problems
  - 48.6% of visits = endodontic or caries problem
  - Caries/endodontic incidence ≈ 2.43 per 100,000 person-days
  - Dental = 6.9-9.3% of all medical evacuations from submarines
  - 13.1% self-reported dental problem during 101-day submergence
- **Useful for IMM:** These are **events per person-day** — directly compatible
  with the Gamma-Poisson schema.

### BAS Antarctic — McColl et al. 2022
- **Source:** [Dentistry where there is no Dentist: A retrospective analysis of
  urgent dental care reported through the British Antarctic Survey Medical Unit
  (BASMU), 2015–2020](https://consensus.app/papers/details/dc0e0cec1d1257c4bbd1a46ffd8b2e54/)
  (McColl et al., 2022, *Primary Dental Journal*, 2 citations)
- **Design:** Retrospective analysis of BASMU dental morbidity database,
  2015-2020.
- **Findings:** Relatively minor conditions most frequent. Training
  non-dentists to manage dental emergencies was key recommendation.
- **Incidence:** Descriptive only; not quantitative rate.

### Czech/Brazilian Antarctic programs — Peřina et al. 2024
- **Source:** [Analysis of dental care in Antarctic
  crews](https://consensus.app/papers/details/c620217eb723527c991bec87575ee55c/)
  (Peřina et al., 2024, *Czech Polar Reports*, 1 citation)
- **Findings:** **Dental problems = 10-15% of medical cases** in overwintering
  crews; <5% in summer-only crews. Data from 15 years Czech and 5 years
  Brazilian Antarctic programs.

## 3. Diarrhea / Gastrointestinal

### Cherry et al. 2024 — Antarctica systematic review
- **Source:** [A systematic review of medical emergencies in Antarctica: lessons
  for long duration
  spaceflight](https://consensus.app/papers/details/0720f756c2c25962b00b8f5922bc618e/)
  (Cherry et al., 2024, *Acta Astronautica*, 4 citations)
- **Design:** All medical emergencies in Antarctica (1772-2022), 384,539
  expeditioners, 149,642 person-years.
- **Findings:** Injury/poisoning = 1 per 357 person-years (most frequent). GI
  emergencies not isolated as separate category.
- **Incidence:** 1 per 357 person-years for most frequent emergency category.
  GI-specific data not extractable from this review.

### Akinsuyi et al. 2024 — Gut permeability in space
- **Source:** [Gut permeability among Astronauts during Space
  missions](https://consensus.app/papers/details/083169c310805dbba93fa447c4f247cf/)
  (Akinsuyi et al., 2024, *Life Sciences in Space Research*, 11 citations)
- **Findings:** Transcriptomic evidence of leaky gut during spaceflight.
  Reduced expression of intestinal barrier genes, goblet cell development
  genes, gut microbiota modulation. Increased Gram-negative pathogens.
- **Incidence:** Mechanism study — no discrete incidence rates for diarrhea.

### General GI
- Diarrhea/GI in the IMM is modeled as a general medical condition. The K15
  reference values are already in imm-priors.json (alpha: 4.77, beta: 107527).
  No new evidence found specifically for diarrhea incidence rates in analog or
  Antarctic settings — this may reflect that GI illness in ICE environments is
  sporadic and not systematically tracked.

## 4. Allergic Reaction (mild to moderate)

### ICE immune dysregulation — Klos et al. 2025
- **Source:** [Effects of isolated, confined and extreme environments on
  parameters of the immune system - a systematic
  review](https://consensus.app/papers/details/89e0a7cb01485efca1603923757e23af/)
  (Klos et al., 2025, *Frontiers in Immunology*, 11 citations)
- **Design:** 140 studies across space missions, bed rest, MARS500, Antarctic
  stations.
- **Findings:** **"Higher rates of infections and allergic responses"** in ICE
  environments. Immune dysregulation consistently observed. Most measures
  returned to baseline post-isolation.
- **Incidence:** Qualitative — no discrete rate. Supports increased
  vulnerability to allergic reactions.

### Antarctic allergic sensitization — Feuerecker et al. 2022
- **Source:** [One Year in the Extreme Isolation of Antarctica—Is This Enough
  to Modulate an "Allergic"
  Sensitization?](https://consensus.app/papers/details/d0a5751cf97859e28d5cb5a9fa562d00/)
  (Feuerecker et al., 2022, *Biomedicines*, 7 citations)
- **Design:** 39 participants across 5 overwintering crews. Multiplex ALEX
  Allergy Explorer (IgE profiling).
- **Findings:** **~1/3 of participants** displayed specific IgEs against
  pollen. Kinetics showed IgE reduction at 9 months, then rebound on return.
  6/10 had highest house-dust-mite IgE during Antarctic stay. **New or
  increased allergic reactions reported after return.**
- **Incidence:** 33% IgE sensitization prevalence; rebound hypersensitivity on
  re-exposure.

## 5. Skin Infection

### Herpes virus reactivation — Shulgina et al. 2025
- **Source:** [Reactivation of latent human intracellular infections during a
  months-long expedition at the Antarctic Vostok
  station](https://consensus.app/papers/details/0aca4f62fe5154179d4a9e7a6b4e316c/)
  (Shulgina et al., 2025, *Scientific Reports*, 3 citations)
- **Design:** 11 participants, 64th Russian Antarctic expedition to Vostok
  station. Plasma, saliva, urine for pathogen DNA.
- **Findings:**
  - 100% showed EBV DNA changes
  - **80% (9/11) HHV-6 shedding** in saliva/plasma
  - **18% (2/11) HSV-1/2 shedding**
  - Latent pathogen reactivation linked to geomagnetic activity and
    psychological state
- **Incidence:** Very high viral reactivation rates during Antarctic wintering.
  Relevant for skin/oral lesions (herpes labialis).

### ICE immune dysregulation — Klos et al. 2025 (also cited above)
- **Findings:** "Higher rates of infections" in ICE environments. The
  immune-dysregulation mechanism supports increased skin infection
  vulnerability.

## 6. Urinary Tract Infection

### NASA astronaut study — Law et al. 2016
- **Source:** [NASA Astronaut Urinary Conditions Associated With
  Spaceflight](https://consensus.app/papers/details/673aacafd8625bff86769b06d12b93e4/)
  (Law et al., 2016, 8 citations)
- **Design:** Review of Mercury through ISS Expeditions 1-38 medical records.
- **Findings:**
  - **9 UTIs during spaceflight** (cumulative over all missions)
  - Urinary retention: 16 cases, **0.018 per person-flight** (Shuttle and ISS
    nearly identical: 0.019 vs 0.021)
  - UTI rate after catheterization: **17% per mission**
  - Urinary retention odds 3× higher with promethazine
  - Female:male retention odds 11:14 (reversed from terrestrial pattern)
- **Incidence:** ~0.018 UTIs per person-flight. For a 6-month ISS mission,
  this is a low absolute rate but clinically significant.

### Sex-dependent outcomes — Reyes et al. 2022
- **Source:** [Assessment of Sex-Dependent Medical Outcomes During
  Spaceflight](https://consensus.app/papers/details/829516b390d85e61aaaa60e0a18fe066/)
  (Reyes et al., 2022, *Journal of Women's Health*, 3 citations)
- **Findings:** PRA model. Higher pEVAC in women driven by partially treated
  UTI. At 182 days: 0.0388 vs 0.0354. At 2.5 years: 0.350 vs 0.228. Resupply
  decreased UTI influence (0.35 → 0.11).
- **Incidence:** Modeled, not observed.

## 7. Influenza / Respiratory Infections

### McMurdo — Warshauer et al. 1989
- **Source:** [Rhinovirus infections in an isolated antarctic
  station](https://consensus.app/papers/details/dec30807555e59a5a9e99240369d42d2/)
  (Warshauer et al., 1989, *American Journal of Epidemiology*, 19 citations)
- **Design:** 36 days (Aug-Oct 1976). 64 wintering vs 136 newly arrived men.
  Virus isolation and serology.
- **Findings:** 39% wintering vs 43% newcomers had colds — essentially
  equivalent. Only rhinoviruses isolated. Transmission slow. Incidence 2×
  higher in smaller living units.
- **Incidence:** ~40% over 36 days during relief period. Near zero during
  closed winter.
- **DOI:** 10.1093/oxfordjournals.aje.a115236 (est.)

### Australian Antarctic — Cameron et al. 1968
- **Source:** [The epidemiology of respiratory infection in an isolated
  Antarctic community](https://consensus.app/papers/details/698711bd6f6859879bb738d90f065f66/)
  (Cameron et al., 1968, *Journal of Hygiene*, 41 citations)
- **Findings:** Classic pattern: outbreaks on ship assembly, burn out en route,
  few cases during winter isolation, new infections on relief arrival but
  **mild symptoms in acclimatized party**. Apparent heightened resistance
  during winter.

### BAS COVID-era — Boys et al. 2024
- **Source:** [Impact of COVID-19 restrictions on the incidence of respiratory
  viruses in two British Antarctic Survey
  bases](https://consensus.app/papers/details/124098b4465850dfaa91b1d2e9a42449/)
  (Boys et al., 2024, *The Journal of Infection*, 0 citations)
- **Findings:** 251 swabs from BAS bases (Rothera, KEP). Only 2 HPIV
  positives, both asymptomatic. Zero SARS-CoV-2. Quarantine effective.

## 8. Late Insomnia / Sleep Disturbance

### Antarctic circadian misalignment — Sletten et al. 2022
- **Source:** [The role of circadian phase in sleep and performance during
  Antarctic winter
  expeditions](https://consensus.app/papers/details/df98d59549845e7984d16e24a026516e/)
  (Sletten et al., 2022, *Journal of Pineal Research*, 18 citations)
- **Design:** 51 overwintering expeditioners. Daily sleep diaries, monthly
  performance tests, 48-h aMT6s urine collections for 6 months.
- **Findings:**
  - **34/50 (68%)** had sleep at abnormal circadian phase
  - 18.8% of all sleep episodes misaligned
  - Sleep duration: 7.2 ± 0.8 h avg, but **41.4% < 7 h, 19.1% < 6 h**
  - When misaligned: 6.1 ± 1.3 h vs aligned: 7.3 ± 1.0 h (p < 0.0001)
  - Performance and mood worse when misaligned
- **Incidence:** ~68% circadian misalignment. High prevalence.

### Antarctic sleep review — Pattyn et al. 2018
- **Source:** [From the midnight sun to the longest night: Sleep in
  Antarctica](https://consensus.app/papers/details/14843d1fdc3b57f8ab1ed98d4ace1fba/)
  (Pattyn et al., 2018, *Sleep Medicine Reviews*, 46 citations)
- **Findings:** Consistent changes: circadian phase delay, poor subjective
  sleep quality, increased sleep fragmentation, decreased slow-wave sleep.
  Sleep disturbances = main health complaint from Antarctic personnel.

### Korean Antarctic — Kang et al. 2022 (also cited above)
- **Findings:** 8.0% diagnosed with insomnia or other mental disorders. ISI and
  PSQI scores increased significantly.

### MARS500 — Basner et al. 2014 (also cited above)
- **Findings:** 1/6 crew developed persistent sleep-onset insomnia leading to
  chronic partial sleep deprivation, elevated daytime tiredness, and frequent
  PVT-B deficits.

## 9. Indigestion / Dyspepsia

### Cherry et al. 2024 — Antarctica systematic review (also cited above)
- **Findings:** No specific GI/dyspepsia rate extractable. Injury/poisoning
  most frequent emergency category: 1 per 357 person-years.

### Gut permeability — Akinsuyi et al. 2024 (also cited above)
- **Findings:** Leaky gut mechanism in spaceflight. Not specific to dyspepsia.

**Summary:** Indigestion/dyspepsia specifically has very sparse literature for
ICE environments. The IMM K15 priors (alpha: 3.83, beta: 150000) may need to
stand without dedicated evidence rows.

## 10. Headache (CO₂-induced) — Additional

While not in the original zero-evidence list, headache-CO2-induced is relevant
for closed habitats (submarines, spacecraft, Antarctic stations):

### Law et al. 2014 — ISS CO₂ and headache
- **Source:** [Relationship Between Carbon Dioxide Levels and Reported
  Headaches on the International Space
  Station](https://consensus.app/papers/details/1d5f2c319f4358af953bdad7b06ea46d/)
  (Law et al., 2014, *Journal of Occupational and Environmental Medicine*, 139
  citations)
- **Findings:** Each 1 mmHg CO₂ increase → **2× odds of headache**. To keep
  headache risk <1%, 7-day avg CO₂ must be <2.5 mmHg. ISS range: 1-9 mmHg.
- **Incidence:** Dose-response relationship, not discrete rate.

### van Oosterhout et al. 2024 — Space headache
- **Source:** [Frequency and Clinical Features of Space Headache Experienced by
  Astronauts During Long-Haul Space
  Flights](https://consensus.app/papers/details/ed20b869c4fc54ffaf676be626eb58c4/)
  (van Oosterhout et al., 2024, *Neurology*, 3 citations)
- **Findings:** **22/24 (91.7%)** astronauts experienced headache in 3,596
  space days. 378 episodes total (median 9, range 1-128). 89.9% tension-type,
  10.1% migraine. Early-flight migrainous vs late-flight TTH phenotype.
- **Incidence:** ~10.5 headaches per 100 person-days (378 episodes / 3,596
  days).

## Recommendations

1. **Dental conditions** have the strongest quantifiable data (Deutsch 2008:
   events per person-day). Strong candidate for adding evidence rows and
   re-fitting the Gamma-Poisson prior via the PyMC pipeline.

2. **Anxiety** has sufficient Antarctic incidence data (Fukunishi, Kang) to
   construct evidence rows. The 8.0% diagnosed rate from Kang is conservative
   and defensible.

3. **Headache** has excellent spaceflight data (van Oosterhout 2024: 22/24
   astronauts, 378 episodes, person-day denominator). This is currently a zero-
   evidence condition and should be prioritized.

4. **UTI** data is in person-flights, not person-days, making direct
   Gamma-Poisson fitting difficult without conversion.

5. **Sleep/insomnia, allergic reactions, skin infections** have strong
   qualitative evidence but less directly usable person-day incidence rates.

6. **Diarrhea, indigestion** have the weakest evidence — may need to retain
   existing literature-based priors.
