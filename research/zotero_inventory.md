## Methodology

The 288 items are the deduplicated union of eight Zotero searches over Diego's library, each anchored on a different facet of analog-astronaut selection (selection criteria, isolation/confinement, analog missions, medical standards, behavioural health, personnel psychometrics, team performance, pilot aptitude). Two filters were applied. **Central** status is reserved for items whose title, abstract, or publication venue directly addresses *selection* — criteria, instruments, campaigns, medical standards used at selection, or systematic reviews that synthesize them (25 items). **Exclusions** are items whose subject is operationally adjacent but clearly outside the Selectron criterion-design scope — microbiology, plant biology, SANS/microgravity-only ophthalmology, animal models, head-down-bed-rest analog physiology, life-support engineering, radiation environment characterisation (65 items). The remaining 198 are **related** and listed one per line. Where the Zotero `abstract` field is empty, claims were inferred from title + publication + item type and flagged `(abstract not in metadata)`. DOIs are reproduced verbatim from Zotero; never invented. The "cross-agent overlap" section at the end audits coverage against the four already-published evidence tables.

## Central papers (25)

### Inoue & Tachibana, 2013 — *An isolation and confinement facility for the selection of astronaut candidates*

- **Citation:** Inoue, N., & Tachibana, S. (2013). *Aviat Space Environ Med*, 84(8). https://doi.org/10.3357/ASEM.3188.2013
- **Zotero key:** `DXVEMWVY` · **Type:** journal article (PMID 23926666)
- **Selection-relevant claims:** JAXA 5th recruitment, 963 → 10 → 3; one-week isolation-chamber confinement scored by psychologists, psychiatrists, and JAXA managers on leadership, teamwork, productivity; behavioural attitudes argued to require longitudinal observation, not paper testing; expert opinion converged with repeated observation.
- **Implications:** anchors the JAXA ICE-week protocol — primary source for adding an ordinal "longitudinal behavioural observation" criterion separate from interview-only scoring.

### Santy, 1994 — *Choosing the Right Stuff: The Psychological Selection of Astronauts and Cosmonauts*

- **Citation:** Santy, P. A. (1994). Praeger. (abstract not in metadata)
- **Zotero key:** `77D8AWUR` · **Type:** book
- **Selection-relevant claims:** foundational synthesis of U.S. + Soviet astronaut psychological selection through the early 1990s; documents the shift from psychiatric "select-out" (Mercury-Apollo) toward "select-in" personality assessment (Shuttle era); coins the "select-in / select-out" framing later used by NASA BHP and D-MARS.
- **Implications:** historical anchor for the psychological criterion family weights; cited by every subsequent astronaut-selection synthesis.

### Steimle & Norberg, 2013 — *Astronaut selection and training*

- **Citation:** Steimle, H., & Norberg, C. (2013). In *Human Spaceflight and Exploration* (pp. 171–203). Springer. https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-642-23725-6_7
- **Zotero key:** `4MQ8V5PB` · **Type:** book section
- **Selection-relevant claims:** cross-agency review (U.S., USSR/Russia, ESA, CSA, JAXA, China) of campaign structure, criterion families, and class attrition through ~2012; documents diversification from military pilots to engineers/scientists and to gender representation.
- **Implications:** peer-reviewed anchor for the multi-framework comparison currently in `04_existing_frameworks.md`.

### Musson, Sandal & Helmreich, 2004 — *Personality characteristics and trait clusters in final stage astronaut selection*

- **Citation:** Musson, D. M., Sandal, G., & Helmreich, R. L. (2004). *Aviat Space Environ Med*. (abstract not in metadata)
- **Zotero key:** `VT6B88A3` · **Type:** journal article
- **Selection-relevant claims:** one of very few published *final-stage* astronaut-applicant personality datasets — finalists clustered into trait profiles rather than treated as a homogeneous "right-stuff" sample.
- **Implications:** finalist-sample evidence for facet-level (not domain-level) Big-Five encoding in Selectron.

### Mittelstädt, Pecena & Oubaid, 2016 — *Psychometric personality differences between candidates in astronaut selection*

- **Citation:** Mittelstädt, J. M., Pecena, Y., Oubaid, V., & Maschke, P. (2016). *Aerosp Med Hum Perform*. (abstract not in metadata)
- **Zotero key:** `JGPUJAQP` · **Type:** journal article
- **Selection-relevant claims:** DLR-side report on ESA astronaut-applicant psychometric structure; reference for the Temperament Structure Scales + Big Five battery used in the 2008–09 ESA campaign; differentiates advanced-stage from earlier-stage applicants.
- **Implications:** primary source for the ESA personality battery cited in `04_existing_frameworks.md` ESA row.

### Musson, 2016 — *Psychological aspects of astronaut selection*

- **Citation:** Musson, D. M. (2016). In *Aviation Mental Health*. Routledge. (abstract not in metadata)
- **Zotero key:** `ZU6WJSNM` · **Type:** book section
- **Selection-relevant claims:** synthesis chapter for the aerospace-psychiatry audience; covers select-in/select-out, BBI, BHP, and the cross-agency picture; positioned as operator-facing complement to Santy 1994.
- **Implications:** secondary narrative anchor for the psychological / behavioural criterion families alongside Santy 1994.

### Wiedmann, Weerts & Brixius, 2023 — *The ESA Parastronaut Feasibility Project*

- **Citation:** Wiedmann, I., Weerts, G., Brixius, K., et al. (2023). *Sports Medicine*. https://doi.org/10.1007/s40279-023-01891-4
- **Zotero key:** `ND3GPKZC` · **Type:** journal article
- **Selection-relevant claims:** ESA 2021–22 is the first major-agency selection to admit physically impaired candidates; the project asks whether physical performance tests should remain part of recruitment for an inclusive corps and which PPTs are flight-readiness-relevant; sets up methodology across disability categories.
- **Implications:** direct evidence for designing physical-fitness criteria as disability-aware modular criteria rather than universal pass/fail.

### Johnston & Blue, 2021 — *Astronaut Selection: Medical Standards*

- **Citation:** Johnston, S. L., & Blue, R. S. (2021). In *Handbook of Bioastronautics* (pp. 547–561). Springer. https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-319-12191-8_48 (abstract not in metadata)
- **Zotero key:** `9RT9NZS9` · **Type:** book section
- **Selection-relevant claims:** chapter-level summary of NASA medical standards at selection, by two NASA flight surgeons; positions OCHMO standards in operational context; references the Astronaut Medical Board "Select-Out / Select-In" architecture.
- **Implications:** secondary reference behind OCHMO-STD-100.1A for the binary "Select-Out" gating logic.

### Bogomolov et al., 2007 — *ISS medical standards and certification for space flight participants*

- **Citation:** Bogomolov, V. V., Castrucci, F., Comtois, J. M., et al. (2007). *Aviat Space Environ Med*, 78(11). https://doi.org/10.3357/ASEM.2175.2007

- **Selection-relevant claims:** joint NASA / Roscosmos / ESA / JAXA / CSA medical standards for ISS Space Flight Participants — defines a *lower* but still rigorous bar than for professional astronauts; explicitly distinguishes professional from short-duration-participant criteria.
- **Implications:** canonical inter-agency tier between FAA III and OCHMO-STD-100.1A; supports ordinal-floor encoding of medical criteria for analog-grade vs. flight-grade.

### Jennings et al., 2012 — *Flight crew medical standards and spaceflight participant medical acceptance guidelines for commercial space flight*

### Kreykes et al., 2023 — *Selecting Medical Conditions Relevant to Exploration Spaceflight (IMPACT 1.0)*

- **Citation:** Kreykes, A. J., Suresh, R., Levin, D., et al. (2023). *Aerosp Med Hum Perform*. https://doi.org/10.3357/AMHP.6199.2023

### Antonsen et al., 2023 — *Updates to the NASA human system risk management process for space exploration*

- **Citation:** Antonsen, E. L., Connell, E., Anton, W., Reynolds, R. J., Buckland, D. M., & Van Baalen, M. (2023). *npj Microgravity*. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41526-023-00305-z

### Charvat et al., 2022 — *Long-term Cardiovascular Risk in Astronauts vs Cooper Center Longitudinal Study*

- **Citation:** Charvat, J. M., Leonard, D., Barlow, C. E., et al. (2022). *Mayo Clin Proc*. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.mayocp.2022.04.003
- **Zotero key:** `XFSDWQ83` · **Type:** journal article
- **Selection-relevant claims:** matched-cohort comparison of NASA astronauts (selected for "education, unique skills, and health") to the Cooper Center healthy cohort; quantifies the *healthy-worker effect* in astronaut selection — the post-selection population is fitter than the general population, with downstream consequences for risk-stratification.
- **Implications:** empirical evidence that cardiovascular sub-criteria are range-restricted in any future predictive-validity calibration of Selectron.

### Vizel-Parnas, Barnett & Rubinstein, 2020 — *Insights from the analog astronaut selection and team building*

- **Citation:** Vizel-Parnas, N., Barnett, D. L., & Rubinstein, H. (2020). IAC-20,B3,9-GTS.2,3,x60789. (abstract not in metadata)

## Thematic clusters

**Personality-based selection** — splits into (a) astronaut-finalist studies (`VT6B88A3`, `JGPUJAQP`), (b) military aviator personality with predictive validity (`5Y9R6UF5` Barron 2016, `5ZGD6H3Z` Ruff-Stahl NOTECHS 2016), and (c) ICE-personnel personality (Antarctic, analog crews). Heavy reliance on military aviation and Antarctic overwintering samples to bridge the inferential gap to spaceflight; large-N astronaut-applicant personality data are absent.

**Medical screening standards and exploration medical conditions** — dense cluster running from the binding NASA standards (`EIW94P3L`, `MF3DTUW6`) through IPC-grade reviews (`9RT9NZS9`, `KXZWZD8M`, `4BLPQDLR`) to disease-specific exploration-mission risk assessments (`W699U67R` IMPACT 1.0, `MWV2JERN` Roma 2021 BHP impact of medical conditions). Charvat 2022 (`XFSDWQ83`) is the only large-N empirical comparison of selected astronauts to a healthy reference cohort.

**Behavioural health, BHP, and stress under confinement** — anchored on `MWV2JERN` Roma 2021 and `LWJKVRGG` Shea 2009 (NASA BHP Antarctica meta-analysis); supplemented by primary chamber datasets (`MRS49H57` Basner Mars-500, `TL7IUBDA` Dunn Rosenberg HI-SEAS, `STIBXURT` Malpica THOR, `IMBIBXCU` McMenamin AMADEE-18). Mars-500 and Antarctic overwintering provide the highest-fidelity confinement evidence.

**Team performance in confined crews** — anchored on `UA65I4VP` Bell 2019 systematic review and `IMBIBXCU` McMenamin 2020; supplemented by 12+ ICE team-dynamics items including `BJQ46W5J` Roma & Bedwell 2017 (key factors and threats), `NAEHZYMT` Harris 2023 (lunar-construction collaboration), `7VKZVSNB` Kjærgaard 2022 (Antarctic team effectiveness). ISS-era partner-agency studies (Kanas et al.) supply the in-flight signal but are not in this corpus.

**Analog mission case studies** — the library's largest cluster (~46 items): HI-SEAS, MDRS, Concordia, HERA, SIRIUS, CHAPEA, Antarctic stations, AMADEE, D-MARS, THOR, HORST, Mars-500, NEEMO. Most are descriptive case studies rather than selection-criterion studies but together they constitute the cross-platform evidence base for Selectron's analog-applicability priors. `XFQVITH8` Cromwell 2021 and `92CG6Z8Z` Pagel 2016 are the two cross-analog review papers.

**Pilot and aviator selection (cross-domain transfer)** — 14 items with the highest-N validation evidence available in any aerospace-related selection literature. Key items: `NSBSHT4M` Harris (central), `STT6AYSR` Bor et al. Pilot Selection, `5Y93ZU6I` Carretta USAF 2000, `ZGD9ERVV` Trent PCSM 2020, `XZESRQIB` Hoermann & Goerke social competence 2014, `SKYKSGBD` Gomes & Dias latent-growth 2015, `KMJZXYKM` Rose UAS/RPA 2013, `JWG6PJ6W` Carretta SO 2015, `5ZGD6H3Z` Ruff-Stahl NOTECHS 2016, `J8GQC83J` / `YDU66RXZ` Jenkins ML 2021/2022, `SZDCPFJ9` IATA 2019.

**Cognitive aptitude and operational performance under stress** — bridges personality and operational-skill clusters. Strongest items are operational: `BVPVBXU4` Ivkovic ROBoT-r 2019 (Canadarm2-style simulation), `6A4GQX6C` Wang Fuzzy-AHP teleoperation 2019. Cognition Test Battery papers are well-represented but largely tool-validation. The library is thin on Wonderlic-style large-N cognitive testing in spaceflight applicants — a gap also flagged in `evidence_tables/psychological.md`.

**Selection methodology and Bayesian / MCDA precedents** — only `6A4GQX6C` Wang 2019 (Fuzzy AHP for astronaut teleoperation) and `WL2C8WVC` Antonsen 2023 (HSRB causal-DAG) are in the corpus; `ZVDV3KKM` Gilkey 2012 (Bayesian risk-assessment of medical events on long-duration missions) is the closest Bayesian-in-spaceflight item. The Bayesian / MCDA methodological base (Saint-Hilary 2017, Mohammadi & Rezaei 2020, Lahdelma & Salminen 2001, Tervonen & Figueira 2008, Li 2020, Waddingham 2016) is **absent from the corpus** — it lives only in `methodology_precedents.md`.
